Rudbar


Rudbar is a region of Kerman, in the southeast corner of the province. It is centered around the major towns of Kahnuj, Manujan, and Qal'eh Ganj.
From an 1894 British revenue list on Rudbar, we see the size and importance of Rudbar, which was divided into parganas (sub-districts). It is worth noting that this list does not include villages in Bashagerd, which during the Qajar period was administratively dependent on Rudbar.


List of the sub-districts of Rudbar, with the maliat [revenue] of each in 1894.


Khanu [Kahnuj] Pargannah

Khanu [Kahnuj], Gvajgun, Gilmird [Gimord], Dashkirdish, Patkhun, Durkun, Murich, Bung, Sargum, Gushmiran [Geshmiran], Dilran, Ab Sarud, Deh Pish, 'Aliabad, Husainabad, Bijnabad, Shahabad, Saidabad, 'Abbasabad, Muhammadabad, Allahabad, Sulah, Miri, Alimabad, Maskhara [Maskhareh], Zamalabad, Deh Nau, Mandust Kushteh, Pulakabad, Gurazabad, Surkh Kaleh [Sorkh Qaleh], Tah Madad, Charmisir, Kalatug, Tah Mai, Masandun, Tambut, Kunar Kalati, Bandburi, Pir Haji, Birguk, Rigmati [Rigmatin], Jaz Murian or Murghab, Ziarat-i-Imam Husain, Hajiabad.

Maliat, 4,000 tumans.


Manujan Pargannah

Manujan, Kundar, Jaghin, Deh Yau, Chiraghabad, Kashit, Gurg, Chah Firuz, Burgah, Tulagir, Bavajgun, Tijdanu [Tejdanu], Kalat-i-Gang, Dashkirdi, Jagistan, Shahkuh, Naudiz, Tahab, Nauzar, Gushad.

Maliat, 3,000 tumans.


Gulashgird Pargannah

Gulashgird, Guravi, Shah Basaki, Turki, Kamzard, Biluk, Husainabad [Hoseynabad], Rustamabad [Rostamabad], Dami, Kakur Panchar, Saidabad.

To the west of Kuh-i-Kaliman and Kuh-i-Shur and south of Kuh-i-Marz. Maliat, 2,500 tumans.


Kuhisan Pargannah

Darogha, Husainabad [Hoseynabad], Hudian, Baghai, Malikabad [Malekabad], Deh Nau [Deh Now], Hastogan, Zamin Buzurg, Javanabad, Haidarabad [Heydarabad], 'Aliabad

Maliat, 2,000 tumans.
